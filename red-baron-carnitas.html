Red Baron Carnitas

Prep Time : 10 Cook Time : 50 Total Time : 60 Difficulty : Easy Servings : 5

Ingredients

2 ~20 oz cans young jackfruit

1/2 medium onion, diced

1/2 cup vegetable broth

2 TBSP low sodium tamari or soy sauce

1 TBSP oil

2 TBSP brown sugar

1 TBSP tomato paste

1/4 cup Maple Syrup

1 TBSP lime juice

1.5 tsp liquid smoke

Spices:

2 tsp ground cumin

1 tsp oregano

1 tsp smoked paprika

1 tsp garlic powder

Directions

According to Baronese folklore, the Red Baron was defeated in a tournament after a sluggish performance in a mounted melee competition. Legend has it that the the Blue Knight - who defeated him - unknowingly feasted on young jackfruit the night before the tournament. Adhering to tradition, the Red Baron overindulged himself on shredded pork. We can only assume the Blue Knight's cook on that fateful evening was a Rogue. Regardless, his sprightly performance won the day.

Preheat your oven to 425 degrees F.
Drain and rinse the jackfruit. Chop the onion. Measure out the spices. Set aside.
Heat 1 TBSP of oil over medium heat in a large skillet. Add the onion and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
Next, add the jackfruit and 1/2 cup vegetable broth. Cover, and simmer over medium heat for 10 minutes.
Add 1 TBSP of tomato paste, 2 TBSP soy sauce/tamari, 1 TBSP oil, 2 TBSP brown sugar, 2 tsp cumin, 1 teaspoon oregano, 1 tsp smoked paprika, and 1 tsp garlic powder to a bowl and mix well.
Remove the cover from the jackfruit and shred it with two forks as well as you can. It should look roughly like pulled pork.
Add the spice mix to the jackfruit and stir well. Continue cooking for 3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
Next, add the lime juice, maple syrup and liquid smoke to the jackfruit. Turn down the heat to low and allow it to simmer for another 10 minutes.
Spread the jackfruit mixture evenly on a baking pan lined with a non-stick baking mat or parchment paper. Bake for 15 minutes at 425 degrees F.
Serve as the base for tacos, a burrito or burrito bowl.
