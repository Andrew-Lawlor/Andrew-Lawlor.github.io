Ground Aurochs Burrito

Rogue

Cook Time : 45 Total Time : 50 Difficulty : Medium Servings : 6

Ingredients

12 oz bag of frozen riced cauliflower

4 oz (half a normal package) cremini (baby bella) mushrooms

1/2 cup Pecans

1/2 yellow onion, diced

1 4oz can diced jalapenos

1 medium ripe tomato, diced (for topping)

shredded lettuce (for topping)

shredded vegan cheddar cheese (for topping)

1 can vegan refried beans.

1 package whole-grain tortillas

1 avocado

1 TBSP oil

2 TBSP soy sauce or tamari

2 TBSP chili powder

1 tsp ground cumin

1 tsp smoked paprika

1/2 tsp ground coriander

1/4 tsp ground black pepper

Directions

The great Aurochs of old were legendary beasts of immense size. It is said that their meat imparted unrivaled vigor to those who consumed it. That's what the legends say, anyways. We'll never know the truth, because all the heroes who ate the aurochs to extinction themselves had their adventuring careers cut short by heart disease.

The spirit of the Aurochs can live on in your kitchen. Your quest is to deftly transform the humble cauliflower into a ground-meat mixture that will fool even the most stouthearted warrior - but it won't clog his or her arteries.

While chopping and preparing vegetables, preheat your oven to 350 degrees F.

Add pecans to a food processor or blender with a dry attachment and pulse until finely chopped. Don't turn them into dust! Repeat this process for the mushrooms, but make sure to remove their stems first. Chopping them into a few pieces before placing them into the blender will help to ensure consistency.

Dice the onion with a knife or blend/food process until finely diced. Then, drain and rinse the diced jalapenos and set aside.

Next, add pecans, mushrooms, diced onion, jalapenos and frozen riced cauliflower into a large mixing bowl. Pour in the soy sauce and spices and mix thoroughly.

Spread the ground aurochs meat evenly on a baking sheet lined with a baking mat or parchment paper. Make it is no more than 1/2 inch thick. Bake for 30 minutes at 350 F. Remove from the oven and stir. Finally, bake for an additional 15 minutes. Remove from the oven.

Heat up a non-stick skillet over medium-low heat. Craft your burrito by adding refried beans (or mashed pinto beans) and ground aurochs meat to the center of a tortilla. Top with diced tomatoes, vegan cheese shreds and avocado slices. Wrap the burrito. Place on a warm skillet, seam-side down for 2 minutes. Flip over and do the same for the other side. Serve.
