Sweet and Sour Dune Flowers

Mage

Prep Time : 5 Cook Time : 15 Total Time : 20 Difficulty : Easy Servings : 2

Ingredients

12 oz bag frozen cauliflower

1 bag frozen rice

1/2 cup vegetable broth

1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce or tamari

1 1/2 TBSP corn starch

2 TBSP maple syrup

3 TBSP apple cider vinegar

2 heaping TBSP peanut butter

1 tsp garlic powder

1-2 tsp red chili flakes

Directions

In the deserts of the east, a flower grows that can be as soft as clouds or as meaty as mushrooms. The Dune Flower. The desert nomads eat it in abundant quantities, filling their belies with substance and nutrition, but not weighing themselves down in their long and constant journeys. Acquire this flower, frozen if you must, and craft a dish at once sweet and sour to the taste.

In a large mixing bowl, combine vegetable broth, soy sauce, vinegar, maple syrup, peanut butter and spices. Mix well with a whisk until smooth.

Open frozen cauliflower bag and place into microwave-safe bowl. Add a splash of water and microwave on high for 3 minutes. This will thaw the cauliflower and improve the texture.

Heat a pan over medium heat on the stove. Drain any excess water from the cauliflower, then add to the pan. Let it cook for 1-2 minutes.

Add half of the sauce from your mixing bowl into the pan. Stir well, ensuring all of the cauliflower is coated in the sauce. Continue cooking on medium heat for 5 minutes, stirring constantly to prevent the sauce from burning.

Add the 1.5 TBSP of corn starch to the remaining sauce in your mixing bowl. Mix thoroughly with a whisk until there are no clumps of starch.

Add the remaining sauce into the pan and continue cooking and stirring for 2-3 minutes. You will notice the sauce thickening over time. Remove from heat once the sauce reaches your desired thickness (no more than 5 minutes).

Heat the bag of frozen rice in the microwave according to package directions. Serve cauliflower over a bed of rice, and drizzle extra sauce on top.
