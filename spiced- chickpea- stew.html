Spiced Chickpea Stew

Ranger

Prep Time : 5 Cook Time : 20 Total Time : 25 Difficulty : Easy Servings : 6

Ingredients

1 15oz can chickpeas

1 russet potato, cubed

1 medium red onion, chopped

1 cup shredded carrots

1/2 cup whole green olives

1/4 cup dates, chopped

1 cup vegetable broth

2 tsp ras al hanout

1/2 tsp ground turmeric

1/2 tsp ground cumin

1/2 tsp salt

1/4 tsp black pepper

Directions

Enter any spice stall worth its salt in the Sangal Bazar - and ask for their top shelf spice. Every time, they will hand you a bottle of Ras al Hanout - a lovely sweet and savory spice mixture from the deserts of the Alyablah Caliphate. We will combine this spice mixture with traditional desert ingredients to create a delicious chickpea stew.

Tip: Ras al Hanout is a North African spice mix that is sold at most grocery stores. Try your local organic market or order it online if you can't find it. One bottle will last you awhile!

Measure out your dry spices and put them in a small bowl. This will save time later. Chop the red onion and cube your potato. If you didn't buy pre-shredded carrots, either shred or finely chop them. Pit and chop the dates. Finally, drain and rinse the chickpeas and set aside.

Heat 1 TBSP of oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the onions and saute for 30 seconds. Now, add all of the dry spices and stir. Let this cook for about 3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Your kitchen should smell like the Sangal Bazaar at this point.

Next, add the cubed potato and 1 cup vegetable broth. Stir, then put the lid on your skillet and allow it to cook for ~12 minutes, checking and stirring occasionally. You'll know it's ready when you can easily pierce the potato chunks with a fork.

Remove the lid and add the carrot, chickpeas, olives and dates. Stir, then continue cooking for another 3 minutes.

Taste the mixture, and add salt to taste if needed. Serve on its own, or over rice or couscous.
