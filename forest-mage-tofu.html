Forest Mage Tofu

Mage

Prep Time : 5 Cook Time : 40 Total Time : 45 Servings : 4

Ingredients

1 cup of cooked brown rice (frozen microwaveable rice is a good option)

1 14oz package extra-firm tofu

1 bunch of broccoli, or 1 bag of pre-cut florets.

5 TBSP soy sauce or tamari (low-sodium)

3 TBSP maple syrup

1 TBSP ketchup

1/2 tsp ground ginger

1/2 tsp garlic powder

1/2 tsp onion powder

1 small handful sesame seeds

Directions

The mages of the forest realm have conjured up a way to concentrate plant protein into blocks of flavor-soaking goodness. When combined with the flavors of the east, these blocks of plant protein sing with flavor. Your quest is to lock in this flavor with the heat of your oven, and revel in its crispy glory.

First, preheat your oven to 350 degrees F.

In a large mixing bowl, combine the soy sauce, maple syrup, ketchup, spices and sesame seeds. Mix together with a whisk.

Drain the tofu and cut into ~1/2 inch cubes. Add the tofu to a large Tupperware container with a lid and then pour the sauce over it. Put the lid on - then shake it in all directions for a few seconds to mix thoroughly. If you have patience and foresight, you can let this marinate in the refrigerator for a few hours to amplify the flavor. If not, carry on! It will still be tasty.

In the same mixing bowl you used to make the sauce, add the broccoli florets. If you're using a full head of broccoli, be sure to break/cut it into bite-sized florets. Add 1 TBSP of oil and a pinch of salt. Shake the bowl a few times to mix thoroughly, or stir with a large spoon.

Prepare two baking sheets with non-stick baking mats or parchment paper. Spread tofu cubes onto one baking mat, making sure to leave at least some space between them. On the second baking sheet, spread out your broccoli florets evenly. Place both baking mats into the oven and bake for 40 minutes at 350 degrees F. Serve over rice.
