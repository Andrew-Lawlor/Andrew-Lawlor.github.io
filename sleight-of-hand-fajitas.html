Sleight of Hand Fajitas

Rogue

Prep Time : 5 Cook Time : 15 Total Time : 20 Difficulty : Easy Servings : 6

Ingredients

2 large Portobello mushroom caps, sliced

1 small onion, sliced

2 red bell peppers, sliced

Flour tortillas

1 ripe avocado

1 TBSP low-sodium tamari or soy sauce

1 tsp garlic powder

1 tsp smoked paprika

1/2 teaspoon cumin

1/2 teaspoon ground coriander

1/4 tsp black pepper



Directions

Sautéed sliced vegetables with seared aurochs or poultry slices - also called fajitas - are commonly eaten in the Ronescan Repulic. Rogues and knaves from all corners of Ronesca know that the meat is merely window dressing. The classic Ronescan spice mix is what really carries the day. Thus, a common practice in Ronesca is to swamp earthy, meaty mushrooms in place of the meat, which lets the spices sing. It also saves the cook a fair sum of money. See if you can fool your dinner guests with this traditional Ronescan tactic!

Sliced, the bell peppers, onion and mushrooms into long, thin strips. Set aside. Heat 1 TBSP of oil or veggie broth over medium heat in a large sauté pan. Add the peppers and onions to the pan and sauté for about 2 minutes.

Next, add the dry spices - including the garlic powder, cumin, coriander, smoked paprika and pepper. Stir this into the peppers and onions thoroughly. It should coat the vegetables evenly, and begin to stick a bit to the bottom of the pan. Let it sauté for about a minute.

Add the slices mushrooms to the pan, along with the soy sauce or tamari. Stir thoroughly. The combines moisture from the tamari/soy sauce and mushrooms will deglaze the pan. If needed, scrape the bottom of the pan a bit with a wooden or non-metal spoon/spatula. You're releasing flavor! Let the vegetables sauté for about 5-7 minutes, until the mushrooms are soft.

Serve on flour tortillas with sliced avocado and salsa, if desired. Watch your guests as they eat - do they notice anything is amiss?
