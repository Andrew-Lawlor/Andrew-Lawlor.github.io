Two-Handed Giant Burger

Warrior

Difficulty : Easy Servings : 6

Ingredients

1 bag frozen microwaveable brown rice

1 medium sweet potato

1 cup rolled oats (dry)

1 15 ounce can chickpeas

whole-grain burger buns

2 handfuls raw spinach

1 TBSP barbeque sauce

1 TBSP balsamic vinegar

1/2 TBSP low sodium soy sauce or tamari

1 1/2 tsp cajun seasoning

1/2 tsp garlic powder

1/2 tsp smoked paprika

Directions

While observing the dietary habits of hill giants, the northern barbarians got the clever idea of mashing discrete but delicious ingredients together into a patty - and serving it between two slices of bread. Since then, this idea has spread across the land. Even the Elves make burgers now - albeit in such a posh way that they would be indiscernible to the average northern tribesman. Sometimes, the old ways are best. Nothing satisfies like a crude, smashed together burger that is at once sweet, smoky and savory.

Cook microwaveable brown rice according to package instructions. Set aside to cool.

Poke several holes in the sweet potato with a fork. Then microwave for 6 1/2 minutes. Allow to cool for a minute or so. Peel the sweet potato (it should be easy when it's hot), then cut into cubes.

Drain and rinse the chickpeas. Set aside.

In a large mixing bowl, add the cubed sweet potato, one cup of the cooked rice, 1 cup dry oats, chickpeas, spinach, vinegar, barbeque sauce. and all spices. Mash and mix together with a spoon or hand masher. Going into a berserker rage will make this process faster.

Once the mixture is reasonably smooth, form it into patties. Don't be afraid to go big here - but shoot for 4-6 patties.

Heat 1 TBSP of oil in a pan and cook the burgers for 5 minutes on each side on medium heat.

Serve the burgers on toasted buns with traditional toppings - including lettuce, sliced tomato, ketchup and mustard. Don't try to dual-wield this bad boy - you will need two hands to eat it properly.
